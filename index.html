The eating window

You eat between 10:30am and 8pm. That's a ~9.5-hour window — closer to time-restricted eating than to intermittent fasting, which is exactly right for someone training fasted in the morning. Don't shorten it further or you'll under-eat protein.

Gut health rules (Tim Spector approach)

  • 30 plants a week. Track them loosely. Each unique plant — a herb, a spice, a different colour pepper — counts.
  • Fermented foods every day. Kefir in the chia pudding. Kimchi or sauerkraut at lunch. Live yoghurt as a snack base.
  • Polyphenols. Olive oil, dark chocolate (85%+), berries, green tea, nuts.
  • Fibre over the day, not in one hit. The chia pudding is ~12 g already. Spread the rest across lunch and dinner.
  • Minimise UPFs. If it has a long ingredient list and you couldn't recognise half of them, leave it.

Wrist rehab — bilateral tendinitis

The specialist said full rest for a month. Two young kids at home make that impossible. Here's the closest you can get.

The non-negotiables

  • Splint at night, every night. No exceptions. Sleep is when the tendon repairs.
  • Splint during the day during flares. Type with the splint on if needed.
  • No gripping heavy things. Use straps for any pulling exercise.
  • Collagen + vitamin C, 30 minutes before any loading. The 30-minute window matters.

What you can do safely with the kids

  • Keep the wrist neutral when picking up Kira — don't bend it back.
  • Use both arms whenever possible.
  • Carry her with the affected arm in a more supported position — across your forearm rather than at the wrist.
  • If the splint is on, leave it on while you lift her if needed.

Re-introduction protocol

  1. Week 1: Isometric wrist holds. Push hand against a wall in flexion and extension, 5 × 30 sec, twice a day.
  2. Week 2: Light dumbbell wrist curls — 1–2 kg, 3 × 15, slow. Both flexion and extension.
  3. Week 3: Add reverse wrist curls and forearm rotations. Same load, increase reps to 20.
  4. Week 4: Light farmer's carries — 8 kg dumbbells, 30 m, 3 sets.
  5. Week 5+: Slowly re-introduce machine pressing with neutral grip. Stop if symptoms return.

The honest truth on tendinitis

Tendons heal slowly — months, not weeks. The temptation when it feels 80% better is to test it with something heavy. That's how it flares again. The collagen, the splint, the machine work, and the patience are the protocol. There is no shortcut.

Knee protocol — left knee, fat pad impingement

From your 2022 MRI: ACL intact, meniscus intact, joint surface normal. Fat pad impingement + mild patellar tendinopathy + slightly mobile patella. All three respond to VMO and patella tracking work.

The diagnosis in plain English

Your kneecap doesn't track in a perfectly straight line during movement. It glides slightly outwards, pinching the Hoffa's fat pad below the kneecap and irritating the patellar tendon. The fix is strengthening the VMO (inner quad), which pulls the kneecap medially and corrects the tracking. The surgeon prescribed exactly this in 2022. You never followed through. This is your second chance.

Daily VMO and tracking work — 10 minutes, every day

  1. Terminal knee extension (TKE) — sit on a chair, lift the foot until the knee is straight, hold 5 seconds, lower slowly. 3 × 15 each leg.
  2. Wall sit with squeeze — back against wall, knees at 60°, small ball between knees, squeeze and hold 30 sec. 3 sets.
  3. Step-ups (low step, 15–20 cm) — slow, controlled, knee tracking over second toe. 3 × 10 each side.
  4. Glute bridge with knee squeeze — towel between knees, squeeze as you bridge up. 3 × 12.
  5. Side-lying clamshells — band above the knees, 3 × 15 each side.

What to avoid — permanently

  • Running
  • Deep squats under load (cap all knee work at 90°)
  • Lunges with a forward knee track
  • Hockey, football, basketball, tennis — anything with cutting or impact

What's safe and recommended

  • Incline walking
  • Cycling (stationary or outdoor)
  • Swimming (front crawl, not breaststroke kick)
  • Leg press to 90°, hamstring curls, Romanian deadlift, hip thrust

Mobility & desk habits

Evening mobility — 12 minutes, before bed

  1. Thoracic opener over a rolled towel — 2 min. Lie on your back with a tightly rolled bath towel placed across your mid-back, arms out wide or overhead. Breathe slowly. This is the single highest-impact stretch in the routine.
  2. Cat-cow — 1 min. Slow, breath-led.
  3. Thread-the-needle — 1 min each side. On all fours, slide one arm under the other through the gap.
  4. 90/90 hip stretch — 1 min each side. Front leg bent at 90° in front, back leg bent at 90° behind.
  5. Pigeon stretch — 1 min each side. Front shin across the body, back leg straight.
  6. Couch stretch (hip flexor) — 1 min each side. Back foot on low sofa, front foot forward in a lunge.
  7. Doorway pec stretch — 30 sec each side. Forearm on the doorframe, lean through.
  8. Neck releases — 1 min. Slow head circles, ear-to-shoulder holds.
  9. Child's pose — 1 min. Wind-down close.
